1918

16 July

The KRRC were attached to 151st (Durham Light Infantry) Brigade of the 50th (Northumbrian) Division.

October 3rd

Below is a transcription of the war diaries on the day he was killed:

Le Catalet

0015

Battalion orders for operation were issued. Very (?) great difficulty was experienced in moving companies to position of assembly (Order 191, para 4). The ground was entirely unknown and no guides were available. The night was dark with frequent bursts of rain.

0530

Battalion Headquarters moved forward A. 115(?) bq3.

0550

Attacking companies reported in positions of assembly.

0605

ZERO HOUR. Companies advanced to the attack.

0730

First message received from Captain NUTTING OCB (left) coy, reported attack progressing favourably, casualties heavy, reinforcements required.

0740

One platoon, 'C' Coy, under Lieut DAVIES, was left forward to reinforce B coy (Capt NUTTING).

0830

Second message from Capt. NUTTING, 'B' Coy, timed 0730 hrs asked for future help as company reduced to 30 rifles and encountering heavy M.G. fire.

0840 hrs

Lieut PREECE went forward with HQ 'C' Coy one platoon to reinforce CAPT NUTTING

0900 hrs

CAPT HAYHURST - FRANCE
